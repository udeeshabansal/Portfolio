Pfizer Inc. — Integrated Valuation & Corporate Finance Analysis
3-Statement Model · DCF · CAPM · DuPont · EVA · Scenario Analysis · Capital IQ
▾
Objective
Full-scope corporate finance and equity valuation of Pfizer Inc. (NYSE: PFE) to assess intrinsic value and strategic positioning following its $43B Seagen acquisition using institutional-grade data from Capital IQ.
Methodology
Built an integrated 3-statement financial model (FY2022A–FY2027E) with segmented revenue across COVID products, oncology, and established medicines. Conducted DCF with WACC derivation, CAPM cost of equity, and Damodaran ERP inputs. Layered DuPont, EVA, and TSR across 1-, 3-, and 5-year horizons.
Key Findings
  • Cost of Equity 7.26% via CAPM (β=0.62, Rf=4.3%, ERP=5.5% Damodaran); WACC at 8.8%
  • Non-COVID revenue projected 10–12% growth through FY2027E driven by Vyndaqel and Seagen ADC portfolio
  • DuPont revealed FY2023 margin compression was COVID-volume driven, not structural supporting recovery thesis
  • Bear/base/bull scenarios produced price range of $22–$38/share

Reliance Industries Ltd — Multi-Segment DCF & 3-Statement Model
Integrated 3-Statement · Segment Revenue Decomposition · DCF · WACC · Scenario Analysis
▾
Objective
Professional-grade, multi-segment financial model for Reliance Industries. India's largest company by market cap covering O2C, Retail, Jio Digital Services, and Oil & Gas, with full DCF and scenario stress-test.
Methodology
Consolidated 3-statement model (FY2018A–FY2029E) in INR crores from Capital IQ actuals. Revenue decomposed by five segments with individual YoY growth drivers. WACC built from first principles (β=0.24, Rf=7.0%, ERP=6.5%) with 4.0% terminal growth rate aligned to India's long-run GDP outlook.

  • Net income projected INR ~1.16 trillion by FY2029E — 12% CAGR from FY2025A

On Holding AG — FX Risk & Supply Chain Cost Analysis
FX Exposure Modelling · Tariff Cost Quantification · Porter's Five Forces · PESTEL
▾
Objective
Isolate and quantify structural cost risks behind On Holding AG's CHF 40.9M net loss in Q2 2025 — despite 32% top-line growth and design margin-recovery strategies executable without incremental debt.
Methodology
Modelled CHF/USD deterioration (USD 1 = CHF 0.91 to CHF 0.79 over H1 2025). Assessed 90% Vietnam production concentration against US tariff exposure of 20–40% on 1M+ pairs annually. Applied Porter's Five Forces, PESTEL, BCG Matrix, and Value Chain Analysis.
Key Findings
  • Annual tariff exposure estimated CHF 40–60M threatening On's 28% constant-currency growth guidance
  • US R&D hub recommended as natural FX hedge: matching USD cost base to USD revenue stream
  • Vietnam-to-Portugal/Slovenia diversification modelled to eliminate tariff drag via EU free trade access
  • Both solutions modelled as cost-neutral against On's CHF 2.86B 2025 revenue target
